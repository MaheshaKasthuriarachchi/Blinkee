It's Always Darth-Est Before The Dawn

No Escape

"There is no escape," the Dark Lord hissed, brandishing a scarlet arc of light with his ancient weapon.

The young Jedi cowering amid broken blocks of stone knew the Dark Lord spoke the truth; he knew the battle was done. He had fought mightily, just as his Jedi master had taught him, but it wasn't enough. The Dark Lord was stronger.

Now, in the ruins of the ancient temple where the battle took place, the young Jedi would meet his end. If only he could reach his lightsaber, but it was no use. It had flown from his grasp in the midst of the fight and fallen down a dark chasm in the stone floor. It was gone.

The Dark Lord moved closer, swinging his weapon playfully, cruelly. "I thought you might have more fight in you," he taunted, clearly enjoying the game of cat and mouse. "No hunger for the fight, middling skill, weak resolve, non-existent stamina. I deserve a more worthy opponent." He drew closer still.

The young Jedi edged away, half hypnotized by the swinging blade of light and the bloodcurdling, teasing tone of the Dark Lord's voice. He climbed slowly to his feet, limped backward, never tearing his gaze from the Dark Lord. He might as well stop trying to escape and instead await his inevitable end. He was tired, so tired.

The Boot is on the Other Foot

The Dark Lord jerked back with surprise, suddenly finding himself faced with a snarling and beweaponed Jedi. "Impossible. I disarmed you myself." He seemed shaken, surprised by the trickery. He glanced toward the chasm where the Jedi's weapon had fallen, as if to assure himself it had really happened, and that momentary distraction proved his undoing.

Mustering every ounce of the Force left in his command, the young Jedi leaped forward, swinging his toy weapon toward the Dark Lord's throat as he grabbed for the real weapon in his hand. The Dark Lord's grasp tightened around his weapon, but it was too late.

Now armed with what appeared to be two light sabers, the Jedi advanced toward the Dark Lord menacingly, moving in for the kill, ready to dispatch the Dark Lord with his own Sith weapon. "Wait," the Dark Lord pleaded. "I must know. How did you get your weapon back? I've never seen such a technique!"

Resigned to His Fate

One corner of the young Jedi's mouth curled upward in a smile. "This isn't my weapon," he told the Dark Lord triumphantly. "It's a high quality reproduction, a fake. You can buy them in a range of colors and styles, from the classic blade like ours to the more recent double bladed and cross guard styles. You can even opt for a toy swords bulk order if you want, from a fine retailer. There are designs with a single, solid hue or a rainbow chasing light, if you prefer."

"Well played," the Dark Lord conceded slowly, seeming more impressed with the young Jedi's ingenuity than he was displeased by the trickery. He seemed resigned to his fate as the young Jedi drew back his arm for the final blow. "...Perhaps you were more worthy than I thought..."

No Surrender

Something inside him wouldn't allow him to submit.

As he backed away, his foot nudged an object and he glanced briefly down. With start of recognition, he realized what it was. A light up lightsaber, left among the stone ruins of the ancient temple by a child who was, perhaps, pretending to fight as vicious a battle as the one now underway.

Without thinking, the young Jedi dove to the stone floor, snatched up the toy blade and neatly rolled over and back on to his feet in one smooth motion, pressing the realistic button on the toy, lighting up the blade and producing movie-quality lightsaber sound effects at the same time.
